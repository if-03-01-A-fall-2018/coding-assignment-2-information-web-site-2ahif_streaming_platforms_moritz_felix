What Is Streaming?

Streaming Media refers to the simultaneous transmission and playback of video and audio data over a computer network. The process of data transmission itself is called streaming, and transmitted ("streamed") programs are referred to as livestream or short stream.

What is a Containerformat

A container format (from English container 'container') specifies in information technology a data structure with which individual data streams of possibly different formats can be merged to form a data stream (for example a container file) (multiplexing). The possibilities of different container formats are very different. The simplest case is to combine several files into one archive file, such as the TAR format.Based on this, the term "container format" is often used in the sense that the data contained in it make a meaningful whole - for example, combine video data with audio data to make a playable movie or combine textual information with images and other information that they have Composite document. Well-known and often used media c ontainer formats, especially for the common storage of the image and sound data of a movie in a single stream, are MP4 (file extension .mp4), Matroska (.mkv, .mka), DivX (.divx) and the outdated AVI (.avi).

Flash Video .flv

Flash Video (FLV) is an open container format developed by Adobe Systems that is primarily used for Internet video content transfer. Depending on the codec used, it is compatible with Adobe Flash players from version 6 or 7.

MP4

MP4 is a video container format provided by MPEG for MPEG-4 content. The MP4 file format is based on the Apple QuickTime file format. Both formats organize media data in hierarchical and sequential data units. These units are called "atoms," and their TYPE identifiers (moov, trak, mdia, minf, stbl, etc.) are identical. You can record a variety of different media types and save them with structural information and their dependencies.MP4 files can store multimedia content in the form of multiple audio and video tracks as well as subtitles, 2D and 3D graphics. This content can then be played locally using appropriate software or streamed over a network.

MOV

MOV is a file format made especially for the QuickTime Player. That means it works perfectly on Macs. The MOV format was developed by Apple Inc. Later, the largely MP-based MP4 became the international standard. MOV and MP4 have many things in common. Both use the MPEG 4 codec for compression and are therefore interchangeable in the Apple QuickTime environment. MOV is a widely used format today that is supported by many computers and applications, so it can also be used to transfer files.MOV is suitable for video editing and Internet broadcasting because it can customize the primary elements individually.In MOV files, multiple tracks are stored independently. Thus, the sound, the image quality or the image resolution can be changed individually, which makes the editing very easy.

Advantages:The MOV files can be played in different system environments due to their high compatibility and require only a small memory space. They can be used by many different operating systems, including Mac, Windows, iOS and Android.

Disadvantages: MOV uses lossy compression for the video tracks, which means that the video quality is not as high as the original video. In addition, every track (audio, video, text) of the MOV file is encoded with a special codec. An application to play the file must therefore know all these codecs to be able to play them gently and without jerking.

Timeline

1995 - 2004

Early days of the world wide web. Several container formats for streaming the first videos are released. Some sites, like Newgrounds, heavily rely on these container formats to display online video.

2005 - 2010

Mass-streaming services like YouTube and Netflix become massively popular for streaming online video. Broadband penetration increases, allowing significant fractions of the population to stream online video. Macromedia Flash is the most popular format for displaying online video, as it is used by YouTube and many other sites.

2011 - 2016

HTML5 starts to displace Flash. Livestreaming becomes increasingly popular, especially in the form of services like Twitch.tv. Many social media startups integrate the streaming of short segments of video, like Vine and Keek. These are, in turn, integrated into the most popular services like Instagram and Facebook.
